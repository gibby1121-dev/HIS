## Slide 2 — The Problem

# A century of tillage and fertilizer stripped the biology out of the soil

- Continuous-corn Midwestern soils are **biologically degraded** — bacterially-skewed, fungal-depleted.
- This is documented, not opinion: fungal:bacterial biomass falls predictably along the disturbance gradient — **forest > grassland > cropland** — and tillage and nitrogen fertilization specifically suppress fungi.
- Farmers are now buying yield by **renting it from inputs** every year, instead of owning it in the soil.

*Source basis: de Vries et al. 2006; soil F:B biogeography (PMC10597451). HIGH confidence, well-replicated.*

---

## Slide 3 — The Insight

# Soil is a microbiome. We've been treating it like a chemistry set.

- The same principle that governs the human gut governs the soil: **function lives in a diverse, coupled community — not in any single organism.**
- Yet the soil-inoculant market sells **single strains** — the agricultural equivalent of a one-organism probiotic dropped into a community of thousands.
- Up to **~90% of single-strain inoculants fail to establish.** The product washes out. The farmer blames "biologicals."

*Source basis: O'Callaghan et al. 2022 (Soil Use & Management); single-strain establishment failure. HIGH confidence.*

---

## Slide 4 — The Thesis

# We restore the missing half of the partnership — and feed it.

- Decomposition and nutrient cycling run on a **cross-kingdom division of labor**: fungi break down recalcitrant residue; bacteria metabolize the released nutrients and fix nitrogen.
- Degraded soil isn't missing a kingdom — it's missing the **fungal half of that handoff**, and the structure that lets the partnership form.
- Our model is **nurture, not control**: deliver the missing functional partners *and* the habitat that lets them establish. Inoculate + feed.

*This pattern recurs across soil, the cow rumen, ant/termite farms, and food fermentation — convergent evolution onto one solution. Frey-Klett et al. 2011; Worrich et al. 2017; Ma et al. 2013.*

---

## Slide 5 — Why This Is Real, Not Faith

# The biology is settled. The agronomy is not. That gap is the opportunity.

**Settled (HIGH confidence):**
- Degraded cropland is fungal-depleted.
- Single-strain inoculants fail; coupled consortia are the path.
- Fungal residue (necromass) builds the most stable soil carbon (Wang et al. 2021).

**Unproven by anyone — including incumbents:**
- That restoring fungi *causally* lifts yield and carbon **independent of the nutrients applied.**
- No company has run the clean field trial. The category runs on belief.

---

## Slide 6 — The Wedge

# All improvement comes from measurement. The industry can't measure soil health.

- A blood panel works because of **validated reference ranges** — decades of "normal vs. not."
- **Soil microbiome science has no equivalent.** The standard metric (F:B ratio) is method-confounded and a contested predictor of function.
- Whoever builds the **function-anchored soil-biology readout** owns the instrument the entire field needs and cannot currently buy.

*Source basis: Joergensen & Wichern 2018; Rousk & Frey 2015. The measurement gap is real and acknowledged in the literature.*

## Slide 7 — Our Method (Phase 0: Characterization)

# We identify beneficial fungi by comparison — not by extracting single strains.

**Sample three things with one locked measurement stack:**
1. **Degraded target soil** (the patient)
2. **Adjacent undisturbed reference soil** — fencerow/prairie/woodlot, same soil series (the recovered state)
3. **Our bioreactor product** (what we deliver)

**The beneficial guild = the delta:** present in healthy soil AND our product, depleted in degraded soil.

*We never isolate one organism. We let the comparison name the missing functional guild.*
